# Whispers of Verse

Poetry is the most intimate form of writing. It strips away the excess and leaves only the essential — the image, the emotion, the truth beneath the words.

## Finding Your Voice

Every poet starts by imitating. You'll write sonnets like Shakespeare, free verse like Whitman, concrete poetry like Apollinaire. This is necessary. This is how we learn.

But eventually, you must find *your* voice. What images haunt you? What rhythms live in your chest? What do you need to say that no one else can say quite the way you would say it?

## The Revision Process

First drafts are for getting the words down. Revision is where the magic happens. 

Cut the unnecessary. Every word must earn its place. Read aloud — your ear will catch what your eye misses. 

## When Words Fail

Sometimes the most powerful poem is the one that admits its own inadequacy. The poem that breaks mid-line. The poem that says "I cannot say this" but tries anyway.

*That's* when poetry becomes true.
